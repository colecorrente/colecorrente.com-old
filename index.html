about me

At four years old, I persuaded my parents to buy me Lego sets and help me engineer massive configurations. Soon after that, I learned about robotics and commandeered my mom’s camera to document it all. I distinctly remember Steve Jobs announcing the release of the App Store; I was eleven years old. Dreaming of designing my own computer application one day, I taught myself to code. At fourteen, I discovered YouTube and became a YouTube Partner - placing my coding dream on hold.

A year later, promotional videos and animated tutorials interested me while working for Machine Hero, a media consulting firm in Rhode Island. I was given the independence to work/intern on major internet advertising campaigns including the Reebok CHECKLIGHT. My interest in engineering re-emerged at a summer pre-college program nearby at Brown University yet I wanted to experience my undergraduate education in a new part of the country. I traveled to California where I rediscovered my passion for computer science.

I am currently a twenty year old college junior and the first Pomona College student accepted into the dual-degree program at the Thayer School of Engineering at Dartmouth College. I will spend this year at Dartmouth and return to Pomona as a senior and graduate in 2019 with a degree in Computer Science. After graduation, I’ll complete a fifth year of engineering back at Dartmouth and will graduate in 2020 with a Bachelor of Engineering (BE) degree.

Nicholas (Cole) Corrente

  • Dartmouth College Class of 2020
